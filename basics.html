Crochet Basics

Proper techniques and best practices for the perfect crochet piece!

Basic Stitches

Name Steps
1 Chain Stitch
  1. Make a slip knot on your hook.
  2. Loop the working yarn over the hook from back to front.
  3. Rotate your crochet hook by about one quarter turn counterclockwise as you loop the yarn to prepare for hooking it.
  4. To make another chain stitch, yarn over the hook, and draw up a loop. Repeat this as many times as necessary.
2 Slip Stitch
  1. Insert your hook into the spot where you want to crochet the slip stitch
  2. Pull the yarn up through your project.
  3. Draw the newly created loop through the active loop on your hook.
3 Single Stitch
  1. Insert the hook through the first chain. Slide the hook under both loops on the top of the chain.
  2. Wrap the yarn over your crochet hook, and grab it with the hook.
  3. Pull or "draw" the hook and working yarn through the loops. You should now have two stitches or "loops" on your hook.
  4. Wrap the yarn around your crochet hook again, and hook the yarn.
  5. Draw the hook and yarn through both of the loops on the hook.
4 Half Double Stitch
  1. To work the first half double crochet into the foundation chain you will crochet into the chain that is three chains away from your hook.
  2. Yarn over and insert the crochet hook into the stitch.
  3. Yarn over again and pull the yarn through the stitch. You should now have three loops on the hook.
  4. Yarn over one more time and pull the yarn through all three loops.
5 Double Stitch
  1. Begin by crocheting a foundation chain. Yarn over the hook and then insert the hook into the chain.
  2. Yarn over again and then pull the yarn through that third chain from the hook where you inserted your hook. When you have completed this step, you should see three loops on your crochet hook.
  3. Yarn over again. Pull the yarn through the first two of the three loops on your hook. This will leave two loops on the hook at the end of this step.
  4. Yarn over one final time. Pull through both of the loops that are still on the hook.
6 Treble Stitch
  1. Yarn over hook twice, then insert hook into next stitch.
  2. Yarn over hook and draw yarn through stitch - (there are four loops on the hook).
  3. Loop yarn over hook and draw through two loops, (there are now three loops remaining on the hook).
  4. Yarn over hook and draw through two loops.
  5. Again, loop yarn over hook and draw through the last two loops on the hook (there is now one loop remaining.)
